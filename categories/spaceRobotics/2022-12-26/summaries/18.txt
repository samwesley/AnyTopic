block 1:  This article discusses how modern schools must prepare students for a future work environment shaped by Artificial Intelligence (AI), Robotics, the Internet of Things (IoT), 3D Printing, and other technologies, preparing them with skills such as problem-solving, logical thinking, creativity and more. This can be done through phenomenon-based learning which emphasizes on powerful tools at our disposal and flipped classrooms in which teachers come into the class to help solve problems.
block 2: Education must adjust to the Fourth Industrial Revolution and its technology-driven transformation of how we think, work, learn and play. With the right skills, students will be prepared to thrive in a future world where half of current work activities are automated. The need for problem-solving, logical thinking, creativity and collaboration is highlighted by education experts as essential 21st Century skills that should be developed through flipped classroom learning models and blended teaching styles.

Most bizarre robot moments of 2022 – including nanobots that swim inside humans and deadly robot dogs

  • Jacob Willeford
  • Published: 17:03 ET, Dec 22 2022
  • Updated: 17:28 ET, Dec 22 2022
  • Jacob Willeford
  • Published: Invalid Date,

    ADVANCEMENTS in robotics in this century have been significant.

    However, in 2022 alone, there have been some particularly bizarre developments from scientists.

    A robot falcon was created to help prevent bird strikes with commercial aircraft.

    San Francisco also reportedly banned a request for police to let their robot forces deal out lethal punishment to suspects.

    It may feel to some that we're headed toward a dangerous future that movies like The Terminator have always warned us about.

    At least four bizarre robotic achievements this year still had optimistic goals for humanity in mind.

    1. Robot dogs

    Aside from the robot falcon, scientists have continued to develop other animal-based robots for essential functions.

    According to Popular Mechanics, the US military created the US Army robot dog, which is a quadruped robot equipped with a sniper rifle on its back.

    It's called "Vision 60" and was created by Ghost Robotics.

    This sounds incredibly dangerous as is, but it can reportedly only fire at a human operator’s command.

    The mounted sniper can, however, take on targets that are about three-quarters of a mile from it.

    There are precautionary measures put in place as well to manage the firing systems.

    Kyle Mizokami, a military and defense writer, has explained that only a human being can permit the robot dog to fire on targets.

    Even with the human operation, this robot dog likely remains a little unsettlingly to some.

    Vision 60 was even allegedly modeled after another robot called Spot that became internet-famous this year.

    Spot, as opposed to Vision 60, was created with non-lethal assistance in mind.

    It has yellow limbs and is supposed to take over in dangerous situations and help out humans in general.

    According to Popular Mechanics, Spot has allegedly worked at an oil rig, decommissioned nuclear sites and construction sites.

    It even reportedly helped medical staff transport Covid-19 patients.

    Spot has also danced on stages and performed at some theme parks around the US.

    This robot dog is even available for purchase at a staggering $74,500.

    2. Xenobots

    Aside from robotic canines, there have been other advancements in 2022 that are a little more invasive.

    Scientists at the University of Vermont, Harvard University and Tufts University have allegedly made a revolutionary development in nanorobotics.

    They refer to them as "xenobots" and claim them to be the first "living robots."

    Xenobots were also created from frogs.

    They're allegedly a cross between stem cells inside a frog heart and frog skin.

    The researchers referred to them as "programmable organisms" about one millimeter in size, at least according to Joshua Bongard, a computer scientist, and robotics expert who co-led the study at the University of Vermont.

    They reportedly used computer algorithms to direct and guide the xenobots.

    The tiny robots can self-heal, move toward a target and even push items all by themselves.

    3. The SlothBot

    Adding itself to the list of wild 2022 robot creations and robotic animals is the Slothbot.

    It resides in the Atlanta Botanical Garden in Georgia and was designed to collect environmental data.

    The SlothBot can record vital temperature and carbon dioxide levels and is designed as the slow creature to avoid detection from other animals.

    Georgia Institute of Technology designed the SlothBot specifically with a set of large eyes to "find out what is pollinating a genus of rare orchids in the Ecuadorian Rain Forest."

    SlothBots are intended to be very nonintrusive to the natural environment of certain wildlife areas.

    It instead quietly and slowly moves using sensors to collect useful and possibly revolutionary data on plants and animals.

    Although this creation may seem bizarre, it's one of the more passive and purely curious robotic creations of the year.

    4. Russian space robot

    There have also been non-animalistic robot creations this year too, including Russia's massive space robot.

    It's called Fedor, which stands for Final Experimental Demonstration Object Research.

    Designed in a humanoid form, the bot was said to be created by Russian scientists for emergencies before being co-opted by the Russian space agency Roscosmos in 2019.

    Roscosmos then re-purposed Fedor to to test a new emergency rescue system on the Soyuz 2.1a rocket headed for space.

    It learned how to manage the connection and disconnection of cables along with using a variety of tools, per Alexander Bloshenko, Roscosmos’ director.

    It can allegedly even use a screwdriver and a fire extinguisher.

    Fedor ultimately flew to the International Space Station to be an astronaut assistant to the crew.

    However, the bot found trouble aboard the ISS for being too clumsy with its limbs.

    Roscosmos allegedly plans to adjust and improve Fedor to achieve its goal.
